About Me

Welcome to my academic website! I am a PhD student in Atmospheric Sciences at IISER Bhopal, working in the GMA (Greenhouse gases, Modeling, and Applications) Lab. My research focuses on atmospheric transport modeling of trace gases.

My current work explores the potential of future greenhouse gas emission monitoring satellites to derive the emissions from point sources over the Indian region.

I am passionate about computational atmospheric science and the intersection of modeling, data analysis, and environmental research. I also enjoy photography, cooking, and cinema.

Research Interests: Atmospheric Transport Modeling, Trace Gas Dynamics, Computational Atmospheric Science

Research

My research spans several interconnected areas within atmospheric sciences and computational modeling. Here are my main research themes:

Atmospheric Transport Modeling

Development and application of numerical models to simulate the transport of trace gases through the atmosphere, focusing on understanding dispersion patterns, mixing processes, and long-range transport mechanisms.

Satellite Remote Sensing

Investigation of the distribution of emission patterns using the satellite images such as Sentinel-5p, OCO2, OCO3, etc.
